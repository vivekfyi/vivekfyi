The Digital Garden Philosophy: Files over Databases

Planted: July 9, 2026 • Cultivated: July 9, 2026

Most traditional blogs are structured like publishing houses. They demand polished, reverse-chronological articles that serve as final statements. In contrast, a Digital Garden is a place for thoughts that are constantly growing, pruning, and evolving.

Why Flat Files?

  1. Longevity: A directory of Markdown files will be readable 30 years from now. A WordPress or SQL database requires active maintenance, PHP updates, and hosting servers.
  2. Version Control: Using git allows you to track the exact evolution of your thoughts. Every commit timestamp is a physical record of growth.
  3. Frictionless Writing: You do not need to log into a CMS. You open your local text editor, type, and push.

The Growth Stages

Ideas grow organically:

  • 🌱 Seedlings: Raw thoughts, snippets, quotes, or bookmarks. High noise, low polish.
  • 🌿 Budding: Connecting different seedlings together, adding structure, and refining explanations.
  • 🌳 Evergreen: Highly polished, permanent notes that you return to and update over time.

By sorting your notes by maturity rather than post date, you encourage visitors to browse by depth of thought.
